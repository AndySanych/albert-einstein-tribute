Albert Einstein

Creator of the theory of relativity, revolutionized modern physics.

Here is a timeline of Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Germany, to a Jewish family. Shortly after, the family moved to Munich.
  • 1896 - Leaves Germany to avoid military service and enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich to study physics and mathematics.
  • 1900 - Graduates from the Polytechnic and struggles to find a teaching position, taking temporary jobs as a tutor.
  • 1902 - Begins working at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern, a job that provides financial stability and allows him to continue his theoretical research.
  • 1905 - Publishes four groundbreaking papers, introducing special relativity, explaining the photoelectric effect, describing Brownian motion, and establishing E=mc².
  • 1915 - Develops and publishes the general theory of relativity, which redefined our understanding of gravity.
  • 1921 - Receives the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which helped pave the way for quantum theory.
  • 1933 - Flees Nazi Germany due to his Jewish heritage and political views, moving to the United States, where he worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1939 - Writes a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning about the possibility of Nazi Germany developing atomic weapons, contributing to the start of the Manhattan Project.
  • 1940 - Becomes a U.S. citizen and continues to advocate for civil rights and peace.
  • 1955 - Dies in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving behind a legacy as one of the greatest minds in modern science.

"Einstein's genius and his contributions to science are a lasting legacy to the limitless potential of human intellect. His relentless pursuit of knowledge reshaped our understanding of the universe and continues to inspire generations of scientists and thinkers."

— Former U.S. President Barack Obama
